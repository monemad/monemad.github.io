About Me

Why Software?

My passion for software stems from a high school project in which my team and I build a rocket probe to detect radiation levels at various altitudes. We didn't have the most sophisticated sensors, but I was able to make up for it using software. This concept of using software to compensate for a lack of hardware taught me that software is:

  1. Unlimited in its potential
  2. Extremely accessible
  3. Ubiquitous in its applications

The potential that software has inspires me the more I understand it and the more I apply it in my various works/projects. The accessibilty inherent in software astoundds me. Anyone with access to a computer and internet has the potential to become a stellar software engineer. And the prevalence of software in our day-to-day lives always reminds me of its ubiquity.

Why me?

I spent the past year developing my skills as a full-stack software engineer. I’ve built and deployed 4 full-stack web applications using various combinations of technologies, including Python, Flask, JavaScript, React / Redux, Express, Node.js, SQL, PostgreSQL, SQLAlchemy, Sequelize, Git, Docker, and Heroku. One project that I’m particularly proud of is Programmatic. My team and I used React and our knowledge of data structures to implement Dijkstra’s shortest path algorithm, and used the results of our solution to plot and render a visualization of the graph traversal using React and CSS. Being able to visualize data by combining the various technologies we used inspired me and reaffirmed that software can provide unlimited solutions.

My most recent project, a peer-to-peer recipe sharing site called Recipeer, was built with a complex, 14-table relational database using SQLAlchemy. This allows for implementation of advanced and highly customizable recipe search/filtering. Using React, I was able to render a dynamic, responsive, and intuitive website design that encapsulated the intricacies of the heavy functionality of the website. Being able to apply software to such a specific application demonstrated that it truly is ubiquitous in it’s applications.

My varied background (line cook, sales manager) and the transition I made to enter the tech industry perfectly highlights how accessible software is. I took the initiative to pursue my passions and grew into a full-stack developer. I demonstrated tenacity by completing App Academy, an intense 24-week software engineering boot camp. My thirst for knowledge and my drive to challenge myself in ways I have not before are my primary motivators for pursuing a career in Software Engineering. I believe that I can bring a diverse perspective to my role not typically found in an engineer due to my unique background.
